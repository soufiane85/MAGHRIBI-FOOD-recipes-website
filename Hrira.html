Hrira

Ingredients:

  1. 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for garnish
  2. 12 ounces boneless lamb shoulder, cut into ½-inch pieces
  3. 1 large yellow onion, diced
  4. 1 teaspoon kosher salt, divided, or to taste
  5. 1 ½ teaspoons smoked paprika
  6. 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  7. 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  8. ½ teaspoon ground ginger
  9. ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  10. ¼ teaspoon ground cinnamon
  11. 4 cloves garlic, minced
  12. 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  13. 4 cups chicken broth
  14. 1 (15 ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  15. 2 ribs celery with leaves, chopped
  16. 2 cups water, or to taste
  17. 1 (15 ounce) can chickpeas, drained
  18. ¾ cup green lentils, rinsed
  19. 1 tablespoon all-purpose flour
  20. 2 tablespoons cold water
  21. ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro, divided
  22. ½ cup chopped fresh parsley, divided
  23. ½ cup vermicelli pasta, broken into ½-inch pieces
  24. ⅛ teaspoon cayenne pepper
  25. 1 lemon, juiced

Instructions:

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Heat olive oil in a stock pot over medium-high heat. Add cubed lamb and cook until nicely browned and some of the fat has rendered (about 5 minutes).
  3. Add diced onion with a generous pinch of salt. Cook, stirring often, until onion is soft and lightly browned (4 to 5 minutes).
    • Season with smoked paprika, coriander, cumin, ground ginger, black pepper, and cinnamon.
    • Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant (30 seconds to 1 minute).
  4. Add tomato paste and stir for about 1 minute.
  5. Pour in chicken broth, crushed tomatoes, and celery. Stir until well combined. Add water and bring to a boil over high heat.
  6. Once boiling, add chickpeas and green lentils. Season with salt to taste. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Prepare a slurry: Mix flour with 2 tablespoons of cold water in a small bowl. Drizzle into the soup to thicken.
  8. Bring back to a simmer and stir in half of the cilantro and half of the parsley.
  9. Continue simmering on medium-low heat until meat and lentils are tender and soup has thickened (about 20 more minutes).
  10. Add vermicelli pasta and cook until tender (10 to 15 minutes). Adjust seasoning with cayenne and salt. Stir in remaining cilantro and parsley, then finish with a drizzle of lemon juice.
  11. Serve immediately, drizzled with olive oil.
