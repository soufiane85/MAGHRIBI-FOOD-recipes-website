Briwat

Ingredients:

  1. 1/2 kg (about 4 cups) flour, plus additional if necessary
  2. 1 soup bowl full (about 200 g or 7 oz.) of golden unhulled sesame seeds, toasted
  3. 1 teaspoon ground anise
  4. 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  5. 1/2 teaspoon salt
  6. 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads, crumbled
  7. 1/4 teaspoon Moroccan yellow colorant
  8. Pinch of Gum Arabic grains (gum acacia, mastic) – mix with 1/4 teaspoon of sugar and crush to a powder
  9. 1 extra-large egg
  10. 1/4 cup butter, melted
  11. 1/4 cup olive oil
  12. 1/4 cup vinegar
  13. 1/4 cup orange flower water
  14. 2 teaspoons yeast dissolved in 1/4 cup warm water
  15. 1 1/2 kg (about 3 lbs.) honey
  16. 2 tablespoons orange flower water
  17. 1 1/2 liters vegetable oil, for frying
  18. 1/2 cup golden unhulled sesame seeds, toasted, for decorating

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the dough: In a large mixing bowl, sift the flour. Add the toasted sesame seeds, ground anise, cinnamon, salt, crumbled saffron threads, Moroccan yellow colorant, and powdered gum arabic mixture. Mix thoroughly so the spices and seeds are evenly distributed.
  2. Add the wet ingredients: Make a well in the center of the flour mixture. Add the egg, melted butter, olive oil, vinegar, and orange flower water. Mix gently until combined.
  3. Activate the yeast: In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast in 1/4 cup warm water. Let it sit for about 5 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  4. Form the dough: Pour the activated yeast into the flour mixture. Knead the dough by hand, adding more flour if needed, until it becomes smooth, soft, and slightly elastic. Knead for about 10 minutes.
  5. Let the dough rest: Cover the dough with a clean cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes to allow the yeast to activate further.
  6. Shape the Chebakia: On a lightly floured surface, roll out the dough into thin sheets (about 2–3 mm thick). Cut into strips and fold them into traditional Chebakia shapes (like flowers or knots).
  7. Heat the oil for frying: In a large deep pan, pour the vegetable oil and heat it over medium heat until hot but not smoking.
  8. Fry the Chebakia: Fry the shaped dough pieces in batches until golden brown on both sides. Use a slotted spoon to remove them and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  9. Prepare the honey syrup: In a large pot, warm the honey with the orange flower water over low heat so it becomes more liquid and easy to coat the Chebakia.
  10. Coat the Chebakia: Dip the fried Chebakia into the warm honey syrup for about 2–3 minutes. Ensure they are completely coated. Remove and let them drain.
  11. Decorate: Sprinkle toasted sesame seeds over the coated Chebakia while still warm.
  12. Store: Let them cool completely. Store in an airtight container at room temperature for several days, or refrigerate/freeze for longer storage.
